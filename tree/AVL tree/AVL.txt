An AVL tree is a self-balancing binary search tree that maintains a height difference of at most one between the left and right subtrees of any node.

Properties:
1. Every node has a balance factor (BF) that is the height difference between its left and right subtrees.
2. BF can be -1, 0, or 1.
3. The tree is height-balanced, meaning the height difference between any two nodes is at most 1.
4. The tree is a binary search tree, meaning it satisfies the BST properties.
